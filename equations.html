1. Coupled PDEs

1.1 Laplace equation — soil potential distribution (CP)

$$ \nabla\!\cdot\!\bigl(\sigma_{\text{soil}}\,\nabla\phi\bigr) \;=\; 0 \quad\text{in soil} $$ with boundary conditions $$ \phi = E_{\text{anode}} \quad\text{at remote anode,}\qquad -\sigma_{\text{soil}}\,\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial n}\Big|_{r=r_{\text{pipe}}} = i_{\text{BV}}(\phi) \quad\text{(Butler–Volmer at coating holiday)} $$ The Butler–Volmer current density at the steel/electrolyte interface is $$ i_{\text{BV}}(\phi_s) \;=\; -\,i_0\!\left[ \exp\!\Bigl(\tfrac{\alpha_a F\,\eta}{R\,T}\Bigr) - \exp\!\Bigl(-\tfrac{\alpha_c F\,\eta}{R\,T}\Bigr) \right], \qquad \eta = \phi_s - E_{\text{free}} $$

SOURCE: Brichau & Deconinck (1994) Corrosion 50(1):39–47.

Code: src/hydrogen_diffusion.py → C_H_surface_from_potential uses the holiday potential as boundary condition for H entry.

1.2 Oriani–Fick — hydrogen diffusion in steel wall (with trapping)

$$ \frac{\partial C_L}{\partial t} \;=\; D_{\text{eff}}(T,\sigma_h)\,\frac{\partial^{2} C_L}{\partial r^{2}} + \frac{D_{\text{eff}}\,V_H}{R\,T}\,\frac{\partial}{\partial r}\!\Bigl(C_L\,\frac{\partial \sigma_h}{\partial r}\Bigr) $$ $$ D_{\text{eff}}(T) \;=\; \frac{D_H(T)}{1 + K_{\text{trap}}^{\text{eff}}}, \qquad D_H(T) \;=\; D_{H,0}\,\exp\!\Bigl(-\tfrac{E_a}{R\,T}\Bigr) $$ $$ C_L(r_{\text{inner}},t) \;=\; C_H(E_{\text{pipe}},\,\text{pH})\quad\text{(Langmuir entry)},\qquad \left.\frac{\partial C_L}{\partial r}\right|_{r_{\text{outer}}} = 0\quad\text{(Devanathan no-flux)} $$

SOURCE: Kiuchi & McLellan (1983) Acta Metallurgica; Turnbull (1996) on grain-boundary trap density; San Marchi (2012) on $K_{\text{trap}}$.

Code: src/hydrogen_diffusion.py → D_H, D_eff, C_H_surface_from_potential; discretised via Crank–Nicolson scheme.

1.3 Chen–Sutherby–Xing crack growth PDE

The crack-depth time evolution is governed by the coupled EDOs (Newman–Raju 3D semi-elliptical crack): $$ \frac{da}{dt} \;=\; v_{\text{NNpHSCC}}\!\bigl(K_A,\,\Delta K_A,\,f,\,C_H\bigr),\qquad \frac{dc}{dt} \;=\; v_{\text{NNpHSCC}}\!\bigl(K_C,\,\Delta K_C,\,f,\,C_H\bigr) $$ See Section 3 for the explicit form of $v_{\text{NNpHSCC}}$.

2. Fracture Mechanics

2.1 Stress Intensity Factor — Newman–Raju surface crack

At the deepest point of a semi-elliptical surface crack ($\varphi = \pi/2$): $$ K_I^{\,(A)} \;=\; \sigma_h\,\sqrt{\frac{\pi\,a}{Q}}\;F\!\Bigl(\tfrac{a}{t},\tfrac{a}{c},\,\tfrac{\pi}{2}\Bigr) $$ Shape factor for $a/c \le 1$ (oblong crack): $$ Q \;=\; 1 + 1.464\,(a/c)^{1.65} $$ At the surface end ($\varphi = 0$), include the $M_x$ multiplier: $$ K_I^{\,(C)} \;=\; \sigma_h\,\sqrt{\frac{\pi\,a}{Q}}\;F_{\text{surf}}\!\Bigl(\tfrac{a}{t}\Bigr)\;M_x, \qquad M_x \approx (a/c)^{0.5} $$ Hoop stress in thin-wall pipe: $$ \sigma_h \;=\; \frac{P\,D}{2\,t} $$

SOURCE: Newman & Raju (1981) Engineering Fracture Mechanics 15(1–2).

Code: src/crack_growth.py → K_I_deeppoint, K_I_surfacepoint, delta_K.

2.2 Stress Ratio — pressure cycle

$$ \Delta K \;=\; K_I(P_{\max}) - K_I(P_{\min}),\qquad R \;=\; \frac{K_{\min}}{K_{\max}} \;=\; \frac{P_{\min}}{P_{\max}} $$ For Type-I underload cycles (downstream compressor station), $R_{\text{major}} = 0.55$; for ripple cycles, $R_{\text{minor}} = 0.90$.

Code constants: R_RATIO_MAJOR, R_RATIO_MINOR.

2.3 Residual stress SIF — BS 7910 Annex Q

For as-welded condition with membrane and bending residual stress components: $$ K_I^{\,\text{res}} \;=\; \sqrt{\pi\,a}\,\bigl(\sigma_{\text{res}}^{m}\,Y_m + \sigma_{\text{res}}^{b}\,Y_b\bigr) $$ Weight functions (Shen–Glinka simplified, normalised by $x = a/t$): $$ Y_m \;=\; 1.12\,\bigl(1 - 0.31\,x + 0.09\,x^{2}\bigr), \qquad Y_b \;=\; 0.56\,(1 - 0.30\,x) $$ Total SIF at HAZ: $$ K_I^{\,\text{total}} \;=\; K_I^{\,\text{applied}} + K_I^{\,\text{res}}\quad\text{(in HAZ only)} $$

SOURCE: BS 7910:2019 Annex Q, as-welded residual stress profiles.

Code: src/crack_growth.py → K_I_residual, K_I_total; constants SIGMA_RES_M_FRAC=0.10, SIGMA_RES_B_FRAC=0.30 (fractions of SMYS).

3. NNpHSCC Crack Growth — Chen–Sutherby–Xing

3.1 Combined parameter — per-cycle growth

$$ \frac{da}{dN} \;=\; A_{\text{CF}}\,\bigl(K_{\max}\,\Delta K^{\,2}\,f_{\text{eff}}^{-0.1}\bigr)^{n}\;\Phi_{\text{HE}}(C_H) $$ $$ f_{\text{eff}} \;=\; \max\!\bigl(f,\;f_{\text{crit}}\bigr),\qquad f_{\text{crit}} = 10^{-3}\;\text{Hz} $$ HEDE multiplier (Xing model): $$ \Phi_{\text{HE}}(C_H) \;=\; \Bigl(\tfrac{C_H^{\,\text{bulk}}}{C_H^{\,\text{ref}}}\Bigr)^{n_{\text{HE}}}, \qquad n_{\text{HE}} = 0.88 $$

SOURCE: Chen & Sutherby (2007); Xing model via Sun, Zhou & Kang (2021) JIPR.

Constants: A_CF_BASE=4.0×10⁻¹⁴, N_CF=2.0, N_HE_XING=0.88, F_CRIT_HZ=1.0×10⁻³.

3.2 Variable amplitude loading (Type I underload)

$$ \frac{da}{dt} \;=\; f_{\text{major}}\,\Bigl(\tfrac{da}{dN}\Bigr)_{\text{major}} \;+\; f_{\text{minor}}\,\Bigl(\tfrac{da}{dN}\Bigr)_{\text{minor}}\,F_{\text{INT}} $$ $F_{\text{INT}} = 10$ for Type I underload (Chen 2007). Downstream of compressor stations, this term produces ~10× the CGR of mid-pipe constant-amplitude loading.

Code: src/pressure_spectrum.py → da_dt_variable_amplitude.

3.3 Time integration including model error

$$ \left(\tfrac{da}{dt}\right)_{\!\text{real}} \;=\; \left(\tfrac{da}{dt}\right)_{\!\text{model}}\!\!\cdot\,\varepsilon_{\text{model}},\qquad \varepsilon_{\text{model}} \sim \operatorname{LogNormal}(\mu=1.06,\;\mathrm{COV}=61.2\%) $$

SOURCE: Sun, Zhou & Kang (2021) JIPR — calibrated against 39 full-scale CanmetMATERIALS tests.

4. Crack Dormancy & BS 7910 Coalescence

4.1 Stage I → Stage II transition criterion

A crack is dormant (Stage I, slow dissolution only) unless BOTH: $$ \Delta K \;\ge\; \Delta K_{th}^{\,\text{Stage II}} = 2.2\;\text{MPa}\sqrt{\text{m}} \quad\text{AND}\quad C_H^{\,\text{tip}} \;\ge\; C_H^{\,\text{crit, Stage II}} $$

SOURCE: Zhao et al. (2017) — >95 % of NNpHSCC cracks remain below 1 mm and never transition to Stage II; Shirazi et al. (2024) on dissolution-rate saturation.

Code: src/crack_growth.py → crack_is_dormant.

4.2 BS 7910:2019 Cl. 7.3 — coalescence rule

Two coplanar surface cracks are merged into one if the inter-crack gap $s$ is less than the smaller crack depth: $$ s \;=\; |x_i - x_j| - c_i - c_j,\qquad \text{Merge if}\quad s \;<\; \min(a_i,\,a_j) $$ The merged crack has: $$ x_{\text{new}} = \tfrac{1}{2}(x_i + x_j),\quad c_{\text{new}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(|x_i-x_j| + c_i + c_j\bigr),\quad a_{\text{new}} = 1.05\,\max(a_i,a_j) $$

SOURCE: BS 7910:2019 Clause 7.3 (interaction and re-characterisation of coplanar flaws).

Code: src/crack_colony.py → bs7910_coalescence_check.

5. Crack-Tip Chemistry — Turnbull Acidification

Honest error caught while building this: the published simplification we started from used the wrong ratio (a/c instead of c/a). Elongated cracks have more stagnant electrolyte at the tip, so they must be MORE acidic, not less. Sign was inverted. Corrected formula below.

5.1 Crack-tip pH

$$ \text{pH}_{\text{tip}} \;=\; \text{pH}_{\text{bulk}} \;-\; 0.6\,\log_{10}\!\Bigl(\tfrac{c}{a}\Bigr) \;-\; 0.3\,v_{\text{diss}} \;+\; \text{buffer correction} $$ $$ \text{buffer correction} \;=\; 0.3\,\log_{10}\!\Bigl(\tfrac{C_{\text{HCO}_3^-}}{C_{\text{CO}_2}} + 1\Bigr) $$ Fe²⁺ hydrolysis driving the pH drop: $$ \text{Fe}^{2+} + 2\,\text{H}_2\text{O} \;\rightleftharpoons\; \text{Fe(OH)}_2 + 2\,\text{H}^{+} $$

SOURCE: Turnbull (1993); Shipilov (2002); Chen et al. (1999) Acta Mater. on CO₂/HCO₃⁻ buffering.

Code: src/crack_tip_chemistry.py → crack_tip_pH.

5.2 H entry enhancement at crack tip

$$ C_H^{\,\text{tip}} \;=\; C_H^{\,\text{0}}\,\cdot\,10^{\,0.3\,\bigl(\text{pH}_{\text{bulk}}-\text{pH}_{\text{tip}}\bigr)} $$ A drop of one pH unit (e.g. 6.8 → 5.8) raises $C_H$ by $\sim 2\times$ at the tip via the Volmer–Heyrovsky step.

Code: src/crack_tip_chemistry.py → C_H_entry_corrected.

6. Cathodic Protection & Hydrogen Entry

6.1 Subsurface H concentration from pipe potential

$$ C_H^{\,\text{0}}(E_{\text{pipe}},\text{pH}) \;=\; C_H^{\,\text{ref}}\,\exp\!\Bigl[\tfrac{F\,(E_{\text{free}} - E_{\text{pipe}})}{\beta_H\,R\,T}\Bigr]\;\cdot\;10^{-0.3\,(\text{pH}-7)} $$ with symmetry factor $\beta_H = 0.5$ and a physical cap of 0.1 mol m⁻³ for $\alpha$-Fe at NNpHSCC service conditions.

Code: src/hydrogen_diffusion.py → C_H_surface_from_potential.

6.2 Non-monotonic NNpHSCC CGR factor — asymmetric parabolic model

The relationship between CP potential and NNpHSCC crack growth rate is non-monotone. There is an optimum at $E_{\text{opt}} \approx -750$ mV CSE that minimises CGR: $$ \Phi_{\text{CGR}}(E_{\text{pipe}}) \;=\; \begin{cases} 1 + A_{\!+}\,(E_{\text{pipe}} - E_{\text{opt}})^{2}, & E_{\text{pipe}} \ge E_{\text{opt}}\quad\text{(anodic side)} \\[4pt] 1 + A_{\!-}\,(E_{\text{pipe}} - E_{\text{opt}})^{2}, & E_{\text{pipe}} < E_{\text{opt}}\quad\text{(cathodic side)} \end{cases} $$ Calibrated coefficients: $A_{+} = 816$, $A_{-} = 100$. Reported relative CGR values: $$ \Phi_{\text{CGR}}(-750\;\text{mV}) = 1.0\quad\Phi_{\text{CGR}}(-850\;\text{mV}) \approx 2.0\quad \Phi_{\text{CGR}}(-680\;\text{mV}) \approx 5.0\quad \Phi_{\text{CGR}}(-1100\;\text{mV}) \approx 7\text{–}16 $$

SOURCE: Beavers & Thompson (2006); Parkins (2000); Chen (2007 review). Note: NACE SP0169 −850 mV CSE is the correct criterion for general external corrosion, not for NNpHSCC management.

Code: src/cp_optimization.py → CGR_factor_from_potential.

7. Failure Assessment Diagram & MAOP

7.1 API 579-1 Level 2 FAD curve

$$ K_r(L_r) \;=\; \bigl(1 + \tfrac{1}{2} L_r^{2}\bigr)^{-1/2}\, \bigl(\,0.3 + 0.7\,e^{-0.65\,L_r^{6}}\bigr), \qquad 0 \le L_r \le L_r^{\max} $$ $$ L_r^{\max} \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\Bigl(1 + \tfrac{\sigma_u}{\sigma_y}\Bigr) $$ Assessment point per crack depth $a$: $$ L_r = \frac{\sigma_{\text{ref}}}{\sigma_y},\qquad K_r = \frac{K_I(a)}{K_{\text{mat}}} $$ Acceptable if $K_r \le K_r(L_r)$ and $L_r \le L_r^{\max}$.

SOURCE: API 579-1/ASME FFS-1 Level 2 FAD.

Code: src/fad_assessment.py → fad_curve, fad_trajectory.

7.2 MAOP per code (ASME B31.8 / B31.12)

$$ \text{MAOP} \;=\; \frac{2\,\text{SMYS}\,t}{D}\,F\,E\,T $$ Design factors used: $$ F_{\text{B31.8 Cl 2}} = 0.72,\quad F_{\text{B31.8 Cl 3}} = 0.60,\quad F_{\text{B31.12 H}_2} = 0.72\times 0.54 = 0.389 $$ Numerical example for 24″ OD × 0.500″ WT X65 (API 5L seamless, $E=T=1$): $$ \text{MAOP}_{\text{Cl 2}} = 134\;\text{bar}\quad \text{MAOP}_{\text{Cl 3}} = 112\;\text{bar}\quad \text{MAOP}_{\text{B31.12 H}_2} = 73\;\text{bar} $$

Code: src/fad_assessment.py → maop_comparison; constants F_B31_8_CL2, F_B31_8_CL3, HDF_B31_12.

8. NDE — Probability of Detection & Post-Inspection

8.1 EMAT / TFI Weibull POD

$$ \operatorname{POD}(a) \;=\; 1 - \exp\!\Bigl[-\bigl(\tfrac{a}{a_{90}}\bigr)^{k}\Bigr],\qquad a_{90} = 4\;\text{mm},\quad k = 2 $$ At $a = a_{90}$ the Weibull scale gives $1 - 1/e \approx 63.2\%$, matching the $a_{90}$ definition convention used in PHMSA TVC.

Code: src/pod_ilicurve.py → pod.

8.2 Post-inspection initial-crack distribution (rejection sampling)

The cracks that survive an ILI campaign follow: $$ f_{\text{post}}(a) \;\propto\; f_{\text{prior}}(a)\,\bigl[\,1 - \operatorname{POD}(a)\bigr] $$ Sampled via rejection: draw $a \sim f_{\text{prior}}$, accept with probability $1 - \operatorname{POD}(a)$.

SOURCE: standard ILI post-inspection probabilistic treatment; PHMSA Technology Verification Center guidance.

Code: src/pod_ilicurve.py → sample_a0_post_inspection.

9. Model Structural Uncertainty (Aleatory vs Epistemic)

LogNormal parameters from moments $(\mu, \mathrm{COV})$ used throughout: $$ \mu_{\ln} \;=\; \ln(\mu) - \tfrac{1}{2}\,\sigma_{\ln}^{2},\qquad \sigma_{\ln} \;=\; \sqrt{\ln\!\bigl(1+\mathrm{COV}^{2}\bigr)} $$ With $\mu = 1.06$ and $\mathrm{COV} = 0.612$, the resulting quantiles of $\varepsilon_{\text{model}}$ are: $$ P_{05} \approx 0.36\quad P_{50} \approx 0.90\quad P_{95} \approx 2.29 $$ Aleatory variability (process / parameter scatter) is sampled separately from epistemic uncertainty (the model-form term $\varepsilon_{\text{model}}$).

Code: src/model_uncertainty.py → lognormal_params_from_moments, uncertainty_quantiles, separate_uncertainty_intervals.

10. Bayesian Posterior Update — Particle Filter

After inspection observation $a_{\text{obs}}$, the posterior over the parameter $\theta$ (e.g. initial crack depth) is: $$ p(\theta\,|\,a_{\text{obs}}) \;\propto\; p(a_{\text{obs}}\,|\,\theta)\,p(\theta) $$ Particle filter update of weights given a detection/non-detection event: $$ w_i^{(t+1)} \;\propto\; \begin{cases} w_i^{(t)}\cdot \operatorname{POD}(\theta_i + da/dt \cdot t), & \text{if detected} \\[4pt] w_i^{(t)}\cdot \bigl[1 - \operatorname{POD}(\theta_i + da/dt \cdot t)\bigr], & \text{if missed} \end{cases} $$ Effective sample size monitor: $$ \text{ESS} \;=\; \frac{1}{\sum_i \bigl(w_i\bigr)^{2}} $$

SOURCE: Straub (2004) on inspection-based reliability updating.

Code: src/bayesian_update.py → bayesian_update_particles, multi_inspection_update.

11. Risk-Based Re-Inspection Interval

Total expected cost rate as a function of re-inspection interval $T$: $$ \mathcal{C}(T) \;=\; \frac{C_{\text{insp}}}{T} \;+\; \frac{\operatorname{PoF}(T)\,\text{CoF}}{T} $$ Optimal interval: $$ T^{*} \;=\; \arg\min_{T\ge T_{\min}}\;\mathcal{C}(T) $$ Regulatory check (PHMSA Class 3): $T_{\text{PoF lim}}$ such that $\operatorname{PoF}(T_{\text{PoF lim}}) = \operatorname{PoF}^{\text{reg}}$; require $T^{*} \le 5\;\text{yr}$ for Class 3.

SOURCE: Li et al. (2018); Abubakirov et al. (2020) dynamic Bayesian network for re-assessment.

Code: src/inspection_optimizer.py → optimal_inspection_interval, PoF_from_mc_trajectory.

12. H₂ Blending — KIH Degradation

At pressure $P$ and blend fraction $x_{\text{H}_2}$: $$ K_{IH}(x_{\text{H}_2},x_{\text{CO}_2}) \;=\; K_{IH,0}\,\bigl(1 - \alpha_{\text{H}}\,x_{\text{H}_2}^{\,m}\bigr)\,\bigl(1 + \alpha_{\text{CO}_2}\,x_{\text{CO}_2}\bigr)^{-1} $$ HE index combining H₂ partial pressure and CO₂ impurity: $$ \text{HE index} \;=\; \bigl(\tfrac{P_{\text{H}_2}}{P_{\text{ref}}}\bigr)^{1/2}\;\bigl(1 + 2.42\,x_{\text{CO}_2}\bigr) $$ Resulting MAOP cascade (24″ X65, 0.500″ WT, B31.8 Class 2 base): $$ x_{\text{H}_2}=0\%\;\Rightarrow\;134\;\text{bar},\qquad 10\%\;\Rightarrow\;103\;\text{bar},\qquad 30\%\;\Rightarrow\;77\;\text{bar},\qquad 100\%\;\Rightarrow\;38\;\text{bar} $$

SOURCE: Cui et al. (2024) J. Mater. Res. Technol..
